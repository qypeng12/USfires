Wildfire visualizition & prediction

Motivation

Wildfires are a major source of air pollutants in the United States. Stephens et al. suggested that emissions from wildfires can have important consequences for air quality both regionally and at sites far from the fire, posing serious threats to public health and safety. In the context of global warming, wildfires are expected to be more frequent and severe, and thus of increasing concern to the public and government, especially those in fire-prone regions like western U.S. and wildland-urban interface.
