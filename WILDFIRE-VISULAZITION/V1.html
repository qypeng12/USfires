US Wildfires 1992 - 2015

Select the fire attributes and choose the fire year range to see how fire varies!

Main takeaways (you could verify by tweaking the parameters above!)

1. Observed increase in wildfire activity (both fire counts and areas burned) over the past several decades may be partly attributed to climate change.
2. Ignitions caused by humans account for roughly 84% of all wildfires and 44% of total area burned during the study time period (1992 - 2015), with an average of 40,000 wildfires per year across the United States.
3. The human-caused fire season was three times longer than the lightning-caused fire season.
