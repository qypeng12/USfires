Wildfire visualization & prediction

Observation

Visualize and predict the trend of wildfire behaviors


Goal

The goal of our model is forecasting the number of wildfires in each month of each state. From the previous analysis on seasonality, there is a strong correlation between the number of wildfires and the time of the year, which indicates a logical solution on applying a time series algorithm for the model.

Model Training

For training the model, we have divided data into a train and a test dataset. We have used most of the dataset for training and only the last 10 months for testing. The parameters of the ARIMA model are the number of lag observations, the number of differences, and the size of the moving average window. For choosing the best combination of them, we have calculated the AIC score for as many combinations as possible and picked the lowest of them. For example, for California state, the best combination we have chosen is (p=19, d=1, q=2), which gives us the lowest AIC 3792. In the figure, the orange line represents the actual numbers of wildfires, and the blue line represents our predictions. The graph shows that the two lines for the in-sample dataset are almost overlapped with each other, which means the accuracy on in-sample dataset is good. However, at the same time, the model is less powerful on predicting the peak values than the rest. The future prediction starts in 2015, and the grey color shadow represents the 95% confidence interval of our predictions.

Conclusion

For validation and conclusion, the table shows the predictions for our test set. As we have mentioned above, the model is less powerful in predicting peak values, so for July 2015, December 2015, and January 2016, the percentage of the differences are higher than the rest. In conclusion, our model is feasible for achieving our initial goal, which is estimating the number of wildfires for the future, so that the agency will have a better preparation for protecting the wildfires for the following month. Even though the prediction for peak values can be less accurate than the rest, it is still able to show us the importance of matter.
